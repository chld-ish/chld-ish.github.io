the New School for Social Research

The Human Language and Development Laboratory is an interdisciplinary research lab at the New School for Social Research dedicated to the study of human linguistic and nonlinguistic communication from infancy through adulthood. It is directed by Ruthe Foushee, in the Department of Psychology.

The lab integrates evidence from a range of empirical methodologies (including eyetracking; behavioral experiments conducted in the laboratory, classroom, online, and in the 'field;' analyses of naturalistic behavior and spontaneous language use; and qualitative interviews and survey data), with a focus on approaches that are at once ecologically valid and quantitatively rigorous.

Our research is informed by work in semantics, sociolinguistics, linguistic anthropology, philosophy of language, cognitive development, and machine learning.

Jan 2025

We are accepting abstracts for the second annual interdisciplinary early-career researcher conference.

This year's theme is Language and Method, asking:

  • What are the implications of methodological pluralism in the study of human language? Are any methods truly incompatible?
  • Do methodological commitments divide disciplines? If so, are these divisions over- or under- exaggerated? Where is there common ground?
  • What is the relation between methodology and object of inquiry?

Feb 2024

We are excited to be receiving abstracts for the graduate student conference entitled Language: What is it Good For?

There is still time to submit work that speaks to:
  • Contributors to communicative success & characterizing communicative ‘failure’ or misunderstanding
  • Interlocutor effects in conversation: how do different interlocutors make different conversations possible? How do listeners influence speakers?
  • The interplay of language structure, multimodal cues (e.g., gesture), context, biology, & history in linguistic interpretation, online processing, and language production
  • Competing disciplinary conceptualizations of language (& their implications), with opportunities for interdisciplinary insight.

Jan 2024

Lang students Ana Hafley and Nivedita (Nivi) Gargi will be conducting their senior capstones in the lab. Ana is investigating how self reports of psychedelic experiences can give us insight into how we use language to express 'ineffable' phenomena, and the relation between ineffability and mysticism. Nivi will be collecting and analyzing language portraits from children across New York City.
